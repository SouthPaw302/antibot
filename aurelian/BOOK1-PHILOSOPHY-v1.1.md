# Aurelian — Book I (Philosophy) — v1.1 working draft

Constraint (locked): structure is encoded through **words/particles**, not punctuation.

- `.` allowed only as end-of-utterance.
- `:` allowed only as a lexical binder (e.g., `eta:calma`).
- other punctuation is stylistic only.

---

## 1) Core statement (English)

Aurelian exists to make meaning executable.
Meaning is spoken as structure.
Structure is readable by humans and losslessly parsable by machines.

## 1) Core statement (Aurelian)

Aurelian sta: fa vida exekuta.
Vida sta spra-struktura.
Struktura sta lega huma, sta parse mashina.

---

## 2) The three pillars (English)

1. **Intent is explicit.** Nothing important is implied.
2. **State is declared.** Atmosphere, clarity, urgency are data.
3. **Process is structured.** Aurelian prefers flow over drift.

## 2) The three pillars (Aurelian)

Pilar un:
intent sta exa.
intent ne sta implicit.

Pilar du:
eta sta deklar.
atmo, klar(a), urgi, zen sta data.

Pilar tri:
process sta struktura.
Aurelian voli fluo.
Aurelian no voli drift.

---

## 3) Operators (English)

Core operators (attested):

- `ka` because
- `do` therefore
- `ke` that
- `kon` with
- `san` without
- `no` not
- `na` past, `ta` future, `zi` continuous
- `questa` question marker, `qua` how

## 3) Operators (Aurelian)

loga-partikla: ka do ke kon san no.
tempo-marker: na ta.
aspekt-marker: zi.
questa-marker: questa.
wh-marker: qua.

---

## 4) State as data (English)

State must be spoken.
In v1 we bind declared state using the lexical binder `:`.

## 4) State as data (Aurelian)

eta:calma.
eta:sola.
eta:zen.
eta:urgi.
eta:klara.

---

## 5) Examples (English → Aurelian)

1. I speak with clarity.

- mi spra kon eta:klara.

2. We need peace therefore we build structure.

- ni nesa paca do ni mora struktura.

3. It is not urgent.

- so sta no urgi.

4. How do we connect the network?

- questa qua ni kone e neto.

5. Because the world is clustered we use rules.

- ka mundo sta klustr ni fa regula.

## 6) Minimal technical coinages (proposal list)

To keep “semantic operating language” capability, we likely need a small technical layer.
Proposed roots (approve/rename later):

- exekuta (execute/executable)

- deklar (declare)
- process (process)
- drift (drift)

Where possible, prefer compounds with existing roots (`data`, `koda`, `sistema`, `loga`, `rega/lega`, `regula`).
